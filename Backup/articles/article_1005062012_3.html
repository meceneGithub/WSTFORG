Let Us Put Doubt To Rest Once And For All

by Bishop Robert M Charles, MS, M.Div. Living by Faith Ministry

There are many people who doubt their salvation. To live with this kind of burden and insecurity leaves people weak and ineffective in their Christian walk. Thankfully, there is a word from God in this area.

This entire chapter dwells on the hope of the Christian. Heb. 6:18-20 We are taught here in these verses that we possess a hope, v. 18. This hope is not merely some fond wish or desire. Rather, it is a deep settled confidence based squarely upon the promises and the power of God. We are told that this hope is based in 2 great unchangeable forces:

  1. The Promise of God- He will keep His Word! (V. 18 - He cannot lie!
  2. The Oath of God - He has sworn by Himself that what He promises will come to pass. For instance in a court of law, men make the oath, "so help me God" to call God to witness that what they are about to say is the truth.

Men swear by God because there is none greater. Therefore, if God is going to make an oath, He must swear by Himself, since there is none greater than Himself. In other words, God stakes His reputation and Name on His ability to do what He has said He will do.

He will keep His Word, and His credibility depends upon it. The very fact that Jesus is our Refuge, our Anchor and our Forerunner ought to give us confidence in the area of our salvation and its assurance.

If you have received Jesus, you are headed home. There is no need to doubt, there is no need to be afraid.

Bishop Robert M Charles, MS, M.Div.
