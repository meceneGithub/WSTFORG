2016 Theme - "RUNNING TO WIN"

Since we are surrounded by such a great cloud of witnesses; let us throw off everything that hinders us, run with perseverance and fixe our eyes on Jesus the author and perfecter of our faith. Hebrews 12:1

What does it mean to ‘run the race set before us’? (Hebrews 12:1)

  • What kind of race do believers run?
  • Who sets the race parameters?
  • Is it a race we define and purpose for ourselves?

These questions and many more will be answered during our various sessions of our 2016 Women Seeking the Father Annual retreat where Haitian women will be empowered, encourage and challenge to persevere in their faith, especially in the midst of trials and persecutions.

Athletes in a race are surrounded by rows and rows of spectators, pictured for us as “a great cloud of witnesses.” The “witnesses” of the believers’ race are listed in Hebrews chapter 11, which describes the men and women of God whose faithful lives were recorded in the Old Testament.

These saints persevered despite unimaginable oppression and cruelty (Hebrews 11:33–38) and were commended for their faithfulness; and the point of the passage is that their testimony lives on. Their unyielding faith bears witness to the promises of Jesus Christ, urging us to follow their example and “run with perseverance the race marked out for us” (Hebrews 12:1)

In spite of trials, circumstances, persecution, heart aches, pains, sufferings, hurts, disappointments, let us run with endurance and perseverance.

  • like Abraham when you don’t understand God’s way
  • like Nehemiah when your problems overwhelm you
  • like Joseph when life does not turn out the way you planned
  • like Noah when you wonder if your life really counts
  • like Moses when you are fearful or reluctant

The Annual weekend Conferences are just one way women are encouraged in their pursuit of the father’s heart, they are encouraged to set aside one weekend out the year just to spend with Father to be refresh, revive and renewed as they are surrendered by other godly women challenging them to step out of living a life of spiritual bankruptcy into their inheritance as daughters of most High God.
